Roof Replacement & Repair

The honest answer depends on three things: the age of your roof, the extent of the damage, and what year your roof was originally permitted under the Florida Building Code.

Under Florida Statute 553.844, if your roof was permitted to the 2007 Florida Building Code or later (effective March 1, 2009), you can typically repair more than 25% of the roof without triggering a full replacement — provided the existing roof is under 10 years old, the damage isn't from normal wear and tear, and a licensed contractor confirms in writing that the repair won't compromise structural integrity. If your roof was permitted before March 1, 2009 and has never been replaced, the legacy "25% rule" still applies — meaning if damage or work exceeds 25% of the roof section in any 12-month period, the entire roof must be brought up to current code.

At your free inspection we'll tell you exactly which situation applies to your home and what the smartest path forward is — not what generates the most revenue for us.

The 25% rule is found in Section 706.1.1 of the Florida Building Code — Existing Building, 7th Edition (2020). It historically required that if more than 25% of a roof section was being repaired or replaced within a 12-month period, the entire roof section had to be brought up to current code.

Senate Bill 4-D, signed in May 2022, modified this rule via Florida Statute 553.844(5). For homes whose roofs were permitted to the 2007 Florida Building Code or later (March 1, 2009 onward), the rule no longer automatically requires a full replacement — homeowners can repair the damaged portion only, provided specific conditions are met (roof under 10 years old, damage not from wear and tear, written sign-off from a licensed contractor or engineer).

For roofs permitted before March 1, 2009 with no subsequent replacement, the original 25% rule still applies. Note that the "25%" is calculated on the area being torn off and replaced — not the area being patched. A small leak patch does not count toward the threshold.

Most residential shingle replacements take 1–3 days. Tile and metal tile replacements typically take 3–7 days due to material weight, profile complexity, and required underlayment detail. Commercial flat roofs vary widely based on building size — typically 1–3 weeks. We schedule with a buffer for Florida afternoon thunderstorms and don't rush installation to hit a daily quota. Your job gets the time it needs to be done right.

We pull all required roofing permits with the appropriate Central Florida county or municipality — Orange, Seminole, Osceola, Lake, Polk, Volusia, or Brevard. Florida law requires a permit for any roof replacement (and most significant repairs). Permit management is included in every full replacement; you receive copies of the permit and the final inspection sign-off at project completion.

Never let a contractor skip the permit. An unpermitted roof replacement can void your homeowner's insurance coverage, complicate future claims, create issues at the closing table when you sell the home, and in some cases expose you to fines from the local building department.

Materials & Systems

We install every major roofing system used in Florida: asphalt shingles (architectural, dimensional, Class 4 impact-resistant), clay tile, concrete tile, stone-coated metal tile (Boral Steel, DECRA, Gerard), standing seam metal, and commercial flat systems (TPO, EPDM, modified bitumen). Every system we install carries a valid Florida Product Approval for wind and wind-driven rain resistance. We recommend the right system for your home, budget, HOA requirements, and aesthetic goals — not just the easiest one for us to install.

Stone-coated metal tile uses a structural steel core coated in natural stone granules to replicate the look of Spanish barrel tile, Mediterranean flat tile, or wood shake — while delivering the strength and longevity of steel. It weighs roughly 75% less than concrete tile, so no additional framing is required, and it's Florida Product Approved for high-wind and wind-driven rain resistance.

For Central Florida's storm climate, stone-coated metal tile is one of the smartest long-term roofing investments: 50+ year manufacturer warranties, won't crack underfoot during maintenance, doesn't shatter from hail or wind-driven debris, and qualifies for wind mitigation insurance credits that can meaningfully reduce your annual homeowner's premium.

All three are leading stone-coated steel manufacturers, now operating under the Westlake Royal Roofing Solutions umbrella. Boral Steel offers five core profiles including Barrel Vault (Spanish tile look) and Pacific Tile (Mediterranean flat). DECRA is the original stone-coated steel system with 50+ years of R&D — known for its hidden fastener system and Villa Tile profile. Gerard offers Barrel Vault and Pacific Tile profiles with the broadest color range and strong Florida coastal performance.

All three carry Florida Product Approval and 50-year manufacturer warranties. The right choice depends on your home's architecture, HOA requirements, available color palette, and budget — we walk every client through the comparison at the free inspection.

Every Caliber project includes two separate warranties:

Caliber 5-year workmanship warranty — covers defects in our installation labor for 5 years after project completion. This is our guarantee, separate from the material.

Manufacturer material warranty — covers defects in the roofing product itself, provided directly by the manufacturer (Owens Corning, GAF, Boral, DECRA, Gerard, etc.). Length and terms vary by system: shingles typically 25–50 years, stone-coated metal tile 50 years, standing seam metal 25–40 years, clay and concrete tile often lifetime limited, flat membranes 10–20 years.

You receive full warranty documentation and registration at project completion.

Storm Damage & Insurance

Document what you can safely from the ground — photos and video. Do not climb onto a wet, damaged, or structurally compromised roof. If there's active water intrusion, mitigate further interior damage if it's safe to do so (buckets, tarps over indoor belongings).

Then call us before you call your insurance company. We'll conduct a thorough damage assessment, document everything properly with photo evidence, and help you understand the full scope of damage before you file the claim. This puts you in a far stronger position when the adjuster shows up. Florida law gives you up to one year from the date of loss to file a hurricane claim — there's no need to rush a decision under pressure.

Yes — and this is one of the most important services we provide. We document all damage with photos and a written report, help you file the claim, and physically attend the insurance adjuster's inspection to ensure nothing gets missed or undervalued. When initial settlement offers don't reflect the true scope of damage, we support supplemental claims.

We work with all major Florida carriers including Citizens Property Insurance, Universal Property & Casualty, Heritage Insurance, State Farm, and others. There is no additional charge for our claims support — we work within the scope of your approved claim.

The My Safe Florida Home Program (MSFH), administered by the Florida Department of Financial Services, provides qualifying homeowners with a free wind mitigation inspection and matching grants up to $10,000 for hurricane-hardening home improvements — including roof-to-wall connector reinforcement, roof-deck attachment strengthening, secondary water resistance, opening protection (impact windows, shutters), and reinforced exterior and garage doors.

For the 2025–2026 program cycle, the state matches $2 for every $1 the homeowner invests in eligible upgrades, capped at a $10,000 grant. Low-income homeowners may qualify for the full grant with no matching requirement. Core eligibility includes: an active Florida homestead exemption, an insured home value of $700,000 or less, and a building permit issued before January 1, 2008.

Critical: Do not start work before grant approval — starting early automatically disqualifies the application. Caliber is a participating contractor; contact us to check your eligibility and coordinate with the program.

Often, yes — sometimes meaningfully. Florida insurance carriers offer wind mitigation credits documented on Form OIR-B1-1802 (the standardized wind mitigation inspection form). Credits commonly apply to roof age, roof shape (hip roofs receive larger discounts than gable), roof-to-wall connection type, roof-deck attachment (nail spacing and ring-shank vs. smooth shank), secondary water resistance, and opening protection.

A brand-new roof installed to current Florida Building Code with proper nailing patterns and a secondary water barrier typically maxes out the available roofing-related credits. Industry data from the My Safe Florida Home program suggests homeowners who fully mitigate their homes save on average around 39% on their wind insurance premium. After your new roof is installed, request an updated wind mitigation inspection and submit the new form to your insurer.
